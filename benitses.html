Benitses

A Traditional Note of Corfu

The Village

Located close to Corfu town, Benitses is a small yet well-equipped village that retains its traditional Greek character. Most of the activity is centered along the main road, making it both convenient and peaceful. You can still see old houses and fishermen at the small harbor, along with the ruins of a Roman villa and Roman baths.

The Beach

Benitses offers both sandy and pebbly beaches with crystal-clear waters that deepen abruptly. The largest beach features activities and boat rentals, while smaller beaches nearby are calmer. Next to the main beach, you’ll find a small marina where both fishing and tourist boats dock.

Where to Eat

  • Pizza Kavouras: Ask for Spyros.
  • Oraies Benitses: Ask for Dimitris.
  • Klimataria: Traditional cuisine – try the “Bianco.”
  • Paxinos: Specializes in fish – everything is excellent.
  • Gyros League (Tsikis): Grab-and-go souvlaki.
  • Mini Market Alexandros: Located at the southern end – ask for Alekos.

Attractions

Achilleion: About 4 km away. Best to visit early to avoid the crowds.

Excursions: Local agencies offer 3–4 tour options. Consider a day trip to Ereikoussa if you have time – it’s a paradise.

Entertainment

In the evening, choose between local restaurants or a drink at a bar. While nightlife is not intense, you can enjoy a relaxing evening at Ether, Lotza, or the Marina bar. For cocktails with a luxurious view, visit Agsana Hotel.

Tips

  • Locals are friendly. Don’t be surprised if someone says good morning or even mentions Sonia (my mom), Sofia (my sister), or me!
  • Parking is available 150 meters up the road near the basketball court (“Drafo”), also where the municipal garbage bins are.
  • Please don’t disturb the ducks.
  • A 4.5 km hiking trail starts at the parking lot and loops through shaded forest paths with stunning sea views. Go early to avoid the midday sun.
  • Coffee is excellent everywhere.
