Benitses Village Guide

🏘️ The Village

Just a short distance from Corfu Town, Benitses is a small village that manages to meet every visitor’s need. The village stretches along the main road and offers both comfort and tranquility. Among Corfu’s tourist destinations, Benitses stands out for preserving the character of a traditional Greek village. You can still find old houses and fishermen in the small harbor, as well as ruins of a Roman villa and Roman baths.

🏖️ The Beach

There are multiple sandy and pebbly beaches around Benitses. The largest one offers water activities and boat rentals, while the smaller ones are quieter. The waters are crystal-clear but get deep quickly. Next to the largest beach, there is a small harbor where fishing and tourist boats are moored.

🍽️ Where to Eat

The village has many restaurants and tavernas serving everything from traditional Greek cuisine to Chinese and Mexican dishes.

  • Pizza Kavouras – Ask for Spyros or Gregory
  • Oraies Benitses – Ask for Dimitris
  • Paxinos – Specializing in fresh fish
  • Klimataria – Try the Bianco special
  • GyrosLeague – Souvlaki to go

🏛️ Sights

  • Achilleion Palace – About 4km away by car. Go early before the tourist crowds arrive.
  • Local offices in the village organize various day trips. There are 3-4 recommended tours including cave visits, etc.
  • Recommendation: If you have a few days to spare, take a day trip to Erikousa Island. A true paradise! 😊

🌃 Nightlife

In the evening, you can choose from a variety of restaurants for dinner and several pubs and bars to enjoy music or cocktails. The nightlife is not particularly intense, but there are cozy spots like:

  • Ether
  • Lodja
  • Marina Bar
  • For a premium drink with a view from another world, visit the Angsana Hotel rooftop bar.

💡 Local Tips

  • Locals will likely greet you when passing by and may even ask how Sonia (my mother) or Sophia (my sister) is doing – or if I’m around. Don’t be surprised if someone walks into the house just to say “Good morning!”
  • You can park your car about 150 meters up the hill from the house, near the basketball court – the locals call that area “Drafo.”
  • That’s also where the garbage collection point is – the municipality collects it daily.
  • Don’t disturb the ducks.
  • There’s a hiking trail after the house and beyond the basketball court. You can do a 4.5 km loop through the trees with stunning views over the village and sea. Best to go early in the morning to avoid the harsh sun, especially on the way back.
  • Great coffee is available everywhere in the village.
